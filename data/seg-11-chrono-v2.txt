YEAR 1929 (2 paras)
============================================================
On 24 Oct 1929, the American Wall Street stock market crashed, as nervous investors sold their stocks and the U.S. and World economy slowed and prices of consumer goods fell.  At the same time a major drought occurred in the western half of the United States causing farm crops to fail compounding the economic and banking problems in the country.  North Dakota farmers were especially hit hard by the Depression and prolong drought.  The years 1934 and 1936 were especially dry.  Crops and grasses did not grow.  Hungry cows lost weight and thousands had to be sold and many died of starvation.  Scorching dry winds picked up particles of soil and created “snowdrifts” made of dirt.  The black clouds also appeared in the sky, which were not rain clouds, but instead were of millions and millions of grasshoppers that swarmed all over the land eating any bit of vegetation (plant life) that lay in their path.  Most North Dakota farmers had obtained a mortgage from a bank in order the buy the farmland they were living and farming on.  Thousands of North Dakota farmers lost their farms because they could not grow crops to sell to pay the banks on the loans they had taken on.  During the 1930s, over 121,000 people moved out of the state and 12,308 farms were lost in North Dakota.

In the 1930 U. S. Census William Raymond, wife Anne Elizabeth (Franklin) and their thirty-eight-year-old son Eddie Raymond were living in Arvilla, Grand Forks Co., North Dakota.  Sixty-one-year-old William was no longer working and he and wife Anne were living with their son Eddie who was working as a “labor”.  Two other unrelated men were also living in the house as “roomers” and were both working as “labors”.  William Raymond no longer was a “Grain Buyer” who stored harvested grain in grain elevators for later sale.  The Great Depression of 1929, had major impacts on the price of farm grain products in North Dakota and many private grain elevators were put out of business.

YEAR 1932 (3 paras)
============================================================
As a rural farming state, North Dakota suffered greatly when the prices received for farm produce declined.  The search for a solution brought about different movements in the 1920s and 1930s.  Farmers banded together to market their produce and reduce the costs of farming.  A “North Dakota Farmers Union” was formed and quickly spread across the state’s prairies.  The local Farmers Unions, built grain elevators and organized petroleum cooperatives that served the needs of an increasingly mechanized rural farming economy.  In 1932, the cooperative group helped form a militant defense organization the “Farmers Holiday Organization”, to take direct action against low commodity prices and farm foreclosures.

The militancy in rural North Dakota farm areas quickly spread into state politics.  A revitalized Nonpartisan League (NPL) emerged in 1932, electing the colorful populist William Langer as the state’s Governor.  William Langer took bold actions when he assumed his Governor’s office in 1933; he slashed state spending, imposed moratoriums on mortgage foreclosure sales, and embargoed shipment of grain from the state.  However, his disregard of law brought Federal investigations, and in 1934 he was convicted of campaign law violations and removed from office; Lt. Governor Ole Olson finished the governor term.  That same year, a divided NPL lost the Governor's election to Democrat Thomas Moodie, who assumed office in 1935, but was quickly disqualified when an investigation discovered that he did not meet state residency requirements.  Lt. Governor Walter Welford succeeded ousted Thomas Moodie, becoming the fourth Governor in seven months.

On 11 Mar 1932, sixty-two-year-old William James Raymond died in Arvilla, Grand Forks Co., North Dakota.  The death certificate for William James Raymond was completed by his wife Anne Elizabeth (Franklin) who did not know the names of Williams’ parents.  Anne Elizabeth may not have been well enough to recall their names for two days later sixty-year-old Anne Elizabeth (Franklin) also died in Arvilla.

YEAR 1945 (1 paras)
============================================================
Finally in the 1940s more favorable weather returned and farm crop yields were profitable again.  With more commodities to sell, farmers benefitted even more from the higher prices stimulated by United States entry into World War II.  Within a span of five years, the farm debt in the state dropped markedly and by the end of the war in 1945 North Dakota residents had accumulated the largest per capita bank deposits in the nation.
